# Plan 10-02: Stops 029 + 030 — Time Dilation + Length Contraction

## Objective

Implement two fully independent Special Relativity simulations. Stop 029 uses the twin paradox scenario: two figures side-by-side with age readouts that diverge live as the user drags a velocity slider from 0 to 0.99c. Stop 030 shows a ruler/train car that visibly squashes horizontally as velocity increases, with a live length readout. Both sims share the same Lorentz factor formula but have no shared code — they are fully independent files that extend their respective existing teasers.

## Context

Stops 029 and 030 are the two halves of Special Relativity's kinematic effects. They must be independent sims per CONTEXT.md — no split-panel, no shared code between the two files.

Critical numerical pitfalls (from RESEARCH.md):
- Velocity slider: use range 0–990, divide by 1000 to get beta. This gives 3-decimal precision. Do NOT use 0–99 divided by 100.
- Clamp: `var beta = Math.min(sliderValue / 1000, 0.9999);` before computing gamma.
- At beta < 0.001: hard-set gamma = 1.000 (prevents `1.00000000001` display from floating-point).
- Gamma formula: `var gamma = 1 / Math.sqrt(1 - beta * beta);`
- Reference values for readout calibration: γ(0.5c) = 1.1547, γ(0.9c) = 2.2942, γ(0.99c) = 7.089.

Stop 029 twin paradox design:
- The key number is the age gap. Show both twins' ages (or elapsed proper times) side-by-side.
- At low v (< 0.1c), γ ≈ 1 and age gap is tiny. The readout must show a non-zero value even here (e.g., "age gap: 0.001 years/year") with a note "essentially no effect at this speed." This prevents users from thinking the slider is broken.
- The traveling twin's clock runs slow by factor γ. If Earth twin ages 1 year, traveler ages 1/γ years. The age gap per year of Earth time is `1 - 1/gamma`.

Stop 030 length contraction design:
- Proper length L0 = 100 m (displayed as a reference).
- Contracted length: `L = L0 / gamma` (equivalently `L0 * Math.sqrt(1 - beta*beta)`).
- At v = 0.99c: L ≈ 14.1 m (the ruler is about 1/7 its rest length — very dramatic).
- The ruler must squash only horizontally (width contracts, height unchanged) — length contraction is along the direction of motion only.
- Show a ghost outline of the rest-length ruler alongside the contracted ruler to make the compression visible.

Both sims must follow established patterns:
- ES5 IIFE, DPR canvas, resize handler, `window.SimAPI` set synchronously, `drawStatic()` last statement.
- Sliders declared in index.html, wired via getElementById in sim.js.
- prefers-reduced-motion: call drawStatic() only, no rAF.
