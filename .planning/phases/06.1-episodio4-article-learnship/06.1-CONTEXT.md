## Implementation Decisions

### A. Project narrative — phases 1–6, not just phase 06

**Decision:** The "El proyecto de ejemplo" section must narrate how the project started (phase 01) and carry through all six completed phases. It should not start at Phase 06.

**Key beats to include:**
- **The project choice:** Why "How Physics Works" was chosen as the example — a real site, 50 stops, 2,500 years of physics, not a toy. Lives in `Episodio4/` in the same repo as the article (meta: building a project about building, documented alongside it).
- **Phase 01 — Foundation & Shell:** The first architectural decisions. The dark luxury aesthetic explicitly requested ("more polished than howaiworks; dark, elegant, rich"). The reference to [encyclopediaworld.github.io/howaiworks](https://encyclopediaworld.github.io/howaiworks/) as the model to beat. All vendor libraries self-hosted, no CDN. The decision to put everything in `Episodio4/` (DEC-001).
- **Phase 02 — Stops Data & Navigation:** `stops.json` as single source of truth for all 50 stops defined from day one. The decision that shapes every subsequent phase.
- **Phases 03–04 — Era 1 & 2 simulations:** RK4 integration for orbital mechanics and pendulums (DEC-006) — not obvious from code alone, the decision prevents a future agent from "optimizing" it away. The 12 interactive Canvas 2D simulations: Thales through Newton.
- **Phase 05 — Polish & Deployment:** GitHub Pages deployment from `Episodio4/` subdirectory. Site goes live.
- **Phase 06 — UX Polish:** The three UAT issues (027 Planck imperceptible animation, 040 Fission chain reaction in milliseconds, search discoverability). Fix plans written from precise root-cause diagnoses, not vague "fix the animation" instructions.

**Narrative frame:** The whole project — all six phases — was built with Learnship. Phases 01–04 used `plan-phase`, `execute-phase`, and `verify-work` from the start; the `discuss-phase` context was written retroactively after execution for those early phases (the project moved fast). That retroactive step is itself a Learnship pattern: the system works even when you enter it mid-project, because the artifacts it generates are valuable regardless of whether they preceded or followed the execution. Phase 05 and Phase 06 ran the full `discuss → plan → execute → verify` cycle in order.

This is not a cautionary tale about skipping steps. It's an honest account of how real projects work — and evidence that Learnship is flexible enough to absorb existing work without requiring a clean start.

### B. DEC-010 — Perlin fBm galaxy background

**Decision:** Include DEC-010 with a focused paragraph, not just a passing reference. It's the best single example of what DECISIONS.md prevents.

**What to convey:**
- The initial approach (stacked radial gradients) produced a result that looked like a colored blob, not a galaxy with filamentary structure
- The decision was to rebuild using per-pixel Perlin fBm noise rendering — more expensive computationally but producing the filamentary nebulae and dust-lane Milky Way feel the site needed
- DEC-010 records not just the choice but the rejection of the alternative — a future agent arriving at `galaxy.js` (or whatever the canvas code is) would see unfamiliar per-pixel rendering and might reasonably propose switching to CSS gradients for performance. DEC-010 preempts that conversation by documenting why gradients were tried and discarded.
- **The insight the paragraph should land:** DECISIONS.md is not a log of what you built. It's a log of what you decided *not* to build, and why. That's the part that context windows forget.

**Length:** One focused paragraph within the section — not a separate subsection.
