Coulomb's Law

Electricity follows an inverse square law — just like gravity

1785 · Charles-Augustin de Coulomb

Coulomb measured the force between electric charges and found it follows the same mathematical form as Newton's gravity — but can attract or repel.

Key Concept
  • F = kq₁q₂/r². Electric force follows the same inverse-square form as gravity — but can be millions of times stronger and can attract or repel.
→ Coulomb showed charges follow a force law. Volta went further — he found a way to sustain that force as a continuous current, inventing the battery and enabling all of electrical engineering.
