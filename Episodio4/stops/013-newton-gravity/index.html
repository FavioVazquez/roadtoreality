Newton's Universal Gravitation

The same force that drops an apple holds the Moon in orbit

1687 · Isaac Newton

Newton's great insight was that the force pulling an apple to the ground and the force keeping the Moon in orbit are the same force — gravity — just acting at different distances. This seems obvious now. In 1687 it was revolutionary: the heavens and the Earth obeyed identical laws.

He illustrated this with a thought experiment: imagine a cannon on top of a very tall mountain. Fire a cannonball horizontally. It falls and lands nearby. Fire faster — it lands farther. Fire fast enough, and the Earth curves away as fast as the ball falls. It never lands. It orbits.

The Inverse Square Law

Newton showed that the gravitational force between two objects is F = GMm/r². Double the distance, the force drops to a quarter. This precise mathematical relationship explained all of Kepler's empirical laws from a single principle.

The "universal" in universal gravitation was the radical claim: the same formula applies everywhere in the universe, at all scales, between all masses. The Moon, Jupiter, distant stars — all governed by the same equation. This was a profound unification of earthly and celestial physics.

Escape velocity — the speed needed to permanently leave Earth's gravity — is about 11.2 km/s. Orbital speed is lower (7.9 km/s) because you don't need to escape, just balance falling with horizontal speed. Try both in the simulation.

Key Takeaway
  • Orbiting IS falling — just with enough sideways speed that the Earth curves away as fast as you fall.
  • F = GMm/r²: gravity follows an inverse square law — double the distance, quarter the force.
  • Newton unified terrestrial and celestial mechanics: the same law governs apples and planets.

$$F = G\frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}$$

→ Newton's mechanics reigned supreme for over 200 years. Physicists built on it to understand heat, electricity, magnetism, and thermodynamics. They thought physics was almost complete. Then things got strange.
