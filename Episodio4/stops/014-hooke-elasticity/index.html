Hooke's Law and Elasticity

Springs, stress, and why things bounce back

1678 · Robert Hooke

Hooke discovered that extension is proportional to force — the simplest law of elasticity, and the basis of every spring and suspension system. His famous anagram, revealed as "ut tensio, sic vis" (as the extension, so the force), gave physics its first linear restoring force law: F = −kx.

Key Concept
  • F = −kx: the restoring force is proportional to displacement. This linear relationship underlies oscillations throughout physics, from pendulums to electromagnetic waves.
  • $$F = -kx$$
→ Hooke's linear restoring force governs solid materials. Next, Bernoulli discovered that moving fluids follow equally elegant rules — and explained why airplanes fly.
