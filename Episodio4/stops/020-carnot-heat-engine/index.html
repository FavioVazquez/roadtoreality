Carnot and the Heat Engine

You can never convert all heat into work

1824 · Sadi Carnot

Carnot proved there is a fundamental limit to how efficiently any heat engine can run — a limit set by thermodynamics itself, not by engineering.

Key Concept
  • No heat engine can be more efficient than a reversible engine operating between the same two temperatures. The Carnot efficiency is η = 1 − Tc/Th — a fundamental thermodynamic limit.
→ Carnot set the efficiency limit. Joule measured the conversion rate between work and heat with precision — establishing conservation of energy as a universal law.
