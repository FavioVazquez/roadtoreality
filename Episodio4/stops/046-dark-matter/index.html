Dark Matter

85% of the matter in the universe is invisible

1970s–present · Vera Rubin

Galaxies rotate too fast for the visible matter inside them to hold together. Something massive and invisible — dark matter — must be providing the extra gravity.

Key Concept
  • Galaxy rotation curves are flat — outer stars orbit as fast as inner ones. Visible mass alone cannot account for this. About 85% of all matter is dark matter — non-luminous, non-baryonic, and unknown.
→ Matter is mostly dark. Even more mysterious, 68% of the universe is dark energy — the force accelerating the cosmic expansion.
