Heisenberg's Uncertainty Principle

The universe has a fundamental limit on what you can know

1927 · Werner Heisenberg

You cannot know a particle's position and momentum simultaneously with arbitrary precision. This is not a measurement problem — it is built into nature.

Key Concept
  • Δx·Δp ≥ ℏ/2. Position and momentum cannot both be known precisely — simultaneously. This is not a limitation of instruments but a fundamental feature of quantum reality.
→ Uncertainty is fundamental. Pauli added another constraint: two identical fermions cannot be in the same state. This rule — not a force — is why matter is solid.
