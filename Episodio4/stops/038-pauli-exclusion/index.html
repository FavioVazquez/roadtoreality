The Pauli Exclusion Principle

Why matter is solid

1925 · Wolfgang Pauli

No two identical fermions can occupy the same quantum state. This rule explains why matter resists compression and why the periodic table has the structure it does.

Key Concept
  • No two identical fermions (electrons, protons, neutrons) can occupy the same quantum state. This rule determines atomic structure, explains chemical bonds, and makes solid matter solid.
→ No two electrons can share a state. Dirac unified quantum mechanics with special relativity — and his equation's solution predicted that antimatter must exist.
