Bernoulli's Principle

Why airplanes fly and curveballs curve

1738 · Daniel Bernoulli

As fluid speeds up, pressure drops. This single insight explains flight, carburetors, and the Magnus effect in spinning balls. Bernoulli derived this from conservation of energy: in a steady flow, the sum of pressure, kinetic, and potential energy per unit volume is constant.

Key Concept
  • P + ½ρv² + ρgh = constant. Where fluid moves faster, it pushes sideways less. This pressure difference is what lifts every aircraft in the sky.
