Boltzmann and Entropy

Why the universe always gets messier

1877 · Ludwig Boltzmann

Boltzmann defined entropy as the number of ways a system can be arranged. His equation S = k log W links microscopic disorder to macroscopic thermodynamics.

Key Concept
  • S = k log W. Entropy measures the number of microscopic ways to arrange a system. The second law says entropy increases because high-entropy states are astronomically more probable.
→ Boltzmann gave entropy a microscopic meaning. Hertz confirmed Maxwell's prediction by creating and detecting radio waves in the laboratory — and in doing so, invented wireless communication.
